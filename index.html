About

I am a Research Assistant Professor at the Biocomplexity Institute & Initiative, University of Virginia, where I work on computational modeling and simulation for infectious disease forecasting and response. My work spans network science, stochastic processes, and — increasingly — agentic AI systems, applied to domains including epidemiology, human mobility, healthy buildings, and neuroscience. I have contributed to national forecasting efforts such as CDC FluSight and the COVID-19 and Scenario Modeling Hubs, and I lead or co-lead several CSTE-, NIH-, and ARPA-H-funded projects on influenza and pandemic forecasting.
