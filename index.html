About

I am a Research Assistant Professor at the Biocomplexity Institute, University of Virginia, where I work on computational modeling and simulation for infectious disease forecasting and response. My work spans network science, stochastic processes, and — increasingly — agentic AI systems, applied to domains including epidemiology, human mobility, healthy buildings, and neuroscience. I have contributed to national collaborative efforts such as CDC FluSight and the Scenario Modeling Hubs, and have led or co-led several CSTE-, NIH-, and ARPA-H-funded projects on infectious disease epidemiology. I was a recipient of the Collaborative Excellence in Public Service Award in 2022 for my work supporting the Virginia Department of Health during its COVID-19 response. I have managed multiple academia-industry partnerships with Google, Metaculus, Kinsa Health, and Signature Science.
